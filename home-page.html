How much do we really care about the wildlife of the Philippines

52,177 Species

Philippines is home to around 52,177 species, half of which is endemic to the Philippines archipelago

About 2000 are critically endangered

"We have over 52,000 species, about half of which are not found anywhere else in the world. Sadly, around 2,000 are either critically endangered or threatened or vulnerable," Lyazaga

DENR 0.46% Budget

Based on the 2023 Philippines national budget of Php 5.28 trillion, DENR the agency that supposes to protect nature only gets P24.08 billion

Wildlife is essential to the Philippines' rich biodiversity, contributing to healthy ecosystems, natural resources, and cultural heritage. With thousands of unique species, the country is a global hotspot for biodiversity, yet many animals face the threat of extinction due to habitat loss, climate change, and human activities. Conservation is critical not only to protect these species but also to maintain ecological balance, ensuring clean air, water, and sustainable resources for future generations. However, limited funding and awareness hinder efforts to safeguard wildlife. By prioritizing conservation, supporting environmental policies, and spreading awareness, we can help preserve the natural world and the vital benefits it provides to both people and the planet.

Endangered Philippine Deer Rescued and Released in Leyte

A Philippine deer, locally known as "bugsok", was rescued in Kananga, Leyte after suffering wounds from illegal hunting traps and dog bites. The Energy Development Corporation (EDC) Biodiversity Team, along with local authorities, treated the deer at the Ormoc City Wildlife Rescue Center before releasing it back into the Tongonan Geothermal Reservation. As an endangered species, the Philippine deer faces threats from habitat loss and poaching, highlighting the urgent need for stronger conservation efforts.

Wounded Philippine Eagle-Owl Rescued in Bukidnon

A Philippine eagle-owl, an endemic and critically endangered species, was rescued in San Fernando, Bukidnon by forest guards from the Philippine Eagle Center (PEC).
